2# Taekwondo Sport Context & Performance Analysis Guide

## Sport Overview

Taekwondo is an Olympic combat sport emphasizing high, fast kicks and jumping/spinning techniques. Originated in Korea, it's now practiced globally with World Taekwondo (WT) as the governing body.

### Match Format

- **Duration**: 3 rounds of 2 minutes each, with 1-minute breaks
- **Objective**: Score points through kicks, punches to body/head
- **Win Conditions**:
  - Most points at end
  - Knockout (KO)
  - Referee Stop Contest (RSC)
  - Opponent disqualification

### Scoring System

- **Body kick**: 1 point
- **Body spinning kick**: 2 points
- **Head kick**: 3 points
- **Head spinning kick**: 4 points
- **Punch to body**: 1 point (head punches not allowed)
- **Technical points** awarded for opponent penalties

Electronic scoring sensors in body protector and headgear automatically register valid kicks.

## World Ranking System

### Ranking Points
Athletes earn points based on:
1. **Competition Level** (Olympic > Worlds > Grand Prix > etc.)
2. **Finish Position** (Gold > Silver > Bronze > Round reached)
3. **Opponent Quality** (beating higher-ranked = more points)
4. **Time Decay** (points expire after 1 year)

### Ranking Importance
- **Olympic Qualification**: Top-ranked athletes get direct slots
- **Seeding**: Higher rank = easier draw (avoid top opponents early)
- **Funding**: Rankings often determine athlete support/funding
- **Competition Entry**: Some events require minimum ranking

### Medal Opportunity Assessment

For each weight category, evaluate:

1. **Current Saudi Athlete Ranking**
2. **Gap to Medal Positions** (Top 8 at major events)
3. **Competition Density** (how many athletes in Top 10)
4. **Ranking Point Availability** (upcoming events)
5. **Athlete Form/Trend** (improving vs declining)
6. **Historical Context** (previous Saudi performance)

**Opportunity Score Formula:**
```
Score = (Base Ranking Score) × (Competition Factor) × (Form Factor)

Where:
- Base Ranking Score = 100 - (Rank × 4)  [Rank 1=96, Rank 25=0]
- Competition Factor = 0.7 + (0.3 × (1 - density/20))
- Form Factor = 0.8 to 1.2 based on recent trend
```
